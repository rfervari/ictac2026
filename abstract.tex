\begin{abstract}
    We study a fragment of the query language XPath featuring data (in)equality operators, and its connection to Description Logics (DLs) with concrete domains. XPath has already been investigated from a Modal Logic (ML) perspective. Given the connection between ML and DL, it is only natural to examine XPath also from the DL viewpoint.
    To naturally capture data comparisons, we resort to DLs with concrete domains. In doing so, we establish an interesting connection between the two universes, from which we obtain transference results (e.g., complexity bounds for reasoning tasks). 
    Furthermore, we refine complexity results and show that concept satisfiability, ABox consistency, and concept satisfiability w.r.t.\ a TBox are all \np-complete problems for the fragment of XPath under consideration. In this way, we obtain a DL fragment with data comparisons that features good computational properties.
\end{abstract}
