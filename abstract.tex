\begin{abstract}
We study a fragment of the query language XPath featuring data (in)equality operators, and its connection with Description Logics (DL) with Concrete Domains. XPath has already been investigated from a Modal Logic (ML) perspective. Given the connection between ML and DL, it is only natural to look at XPath from the perspective of DL.
We resort to DL with concrete domains to naturally capture data comparisons.
In doing so, we  establish an interesting connection between the two universes, from which we can obtain transference results (e.g., complexity bounds for reasoning tasks). 
% and to take advantage of the many computational methods and implementations of DL reasoners to reason in XPath. 
Furthermore, we refine complexity results and show that concept satisfiability, ABox consistency, and concept satisfiability w.r.t.\ a TBox are all \np-complete problems for the fragment of XPath under consideration. In this way, we get a DL fragment with data comparisons featuring good computational properties.
\end{abstract}
